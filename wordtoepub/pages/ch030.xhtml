Regulations in the Banking and Financial Sector

Regulatory frameworks applicable to the banking and financial sector can be classified into three main categories:

  1. General / Management Standards

    1. The cloud service provider must demonstrate compliance with technical requirements and service management capabilities through specific certifications.

  2. Industry-Specific Regulations

    1. Different industries require customized certifications.

    2. Example: A rubber manufacturer needs operational process certifications distinct from those required by an insurance service provider.

  3. Security and Privacy Regulations

    1. Over the years, both European and U.S. regulators have established strict directives on the governance of cloud-native ecosystems.

This classification links regulatory frameworks to financial institutions, considering three levels of compliance scope:

  • Economic-Political Blocks – Regulations imposed by regional economic zones (e.g., EU, US).

  • National Regulations – Country-specific laws applicable to financial institutions.

  • Sector-Specific Regulations – Compliance frameworks tailored for the financial industry, focusing on cloud service adoption.

Economic-Political Level (Supranational Regulations)

These supranational regulations govern business activities across multiple countries or economic regions.

European Union (EU)

  • GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

    • Governs the protection of personal data , including its storage, processing, and transfer in cloud-based financial services.

  • DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act)

    • Aims to strengthen the digital operational resilience of financial institutions , including cloud service providers.

    • Mandates risk management , incident reporting , and business continuity requirements for cloud-based financial services.

  • MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation)

    • Regulates the cryptocurrency market , impacting cloud-based platforms used for digital asset management.

  • PSD2 (Payment Services Directive 2)

    • Enforces security standards for electronic payments and open banking APIs .

    • Introduces strong customer authentication (SCA) and requirements for secure cloud transactions .

  • NIS2 (Network and Information Security Directive 2)

    • Strengthens cybersecurity measures for critical sectors , including banking and cloud services .

    • Applies to all cloud service providers operating in the EU .

    • Requires advanced security protocols , cyber risk management , and business continuity safeguards to protect critical financial infrastructures from cyber threats .

United States

In the United States, financial institutions operating in the cloud must comply with multiple regulations and security frameworks designed to protect customer data, financial integrity, and cybersecurity.

Key U.S. Regulations and Guidelines

  • CSA (Cloud Security Alliance)

    • Provides best practices for cloud security, with specific guidelines for the banking and financial sectors.

  • FFIEC Cloud Computing Guidelines

    • Established by the Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council (FFIEC).

    • Offers security guidelines for adopting cloud computing in financial institutions.

  • GLBA (Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act)

    • Mandates strict data protection standards for financial institutions, including cloud service usage.

    • Requires companies to safeguard customer information against unauthorized access.

  • BSA (Bank Secrecy Act)

    • Designed to prevent money laundering and terrorism financing.

    • Cloud service providers handling financial transaction data must comply with strict reporting and monitoring obligations.

  • SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley Act)

    • Requires strong internal controls for publicly traded companies.

    • Includes compliance mandates for cloud infrastructure used to store financial records.

  • HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)

    • Applies to financial institutions managing healthcare-related transactions.

    • Imposes data protection and encryption requirements for cloud-hosted healthcare information.

  • CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act)

    • Provides GDPR-like privacy protections for California residents.

    • Financial institutions must implement cloud data protection mechanisms to ensure compliance.

  • Regulation S-P (Privacy of Consumer Financial Information)

    • Requires financial institutions to establish security measures protecting non-public personal information (NPI) stored in cloud environments.

  • NYDFS Cybersecurity Regulation

    • Enforced by the New York Department of Financial Services (NYDFS).

    • Mandates robust cybersecurity protocols for financial institutions, including those using cloud services.

  • FedRAMP (Federal Risk and Authorization Management Program)

    • Provides a standardized security framework for authorizing cloud services used by U.S. government agencies.

    • Financial institutions working with federal contracts must use FedRAMP-compliant cloud services.

  • OCC (Office of the Comptroller of the Currency) Guidelines

    • Offers risk management frameworks for banks adopting cloud computing.

    • Focuses on data security, resilience, and compliance with U.S. financial regulations.

National Regulations on Cloud Adoption in the Financial Sector

National regulations govern the activities of financial institutions within specific countries, ensuring compliance with local legal frameworks while aligning with supranational regulations. Below are key country-specific regulations concerning cloud adoption in banking and financial services.

European Union Member States

Italy

  • Banca d’Italia Cloud Guidelines

    • Regulate cloud adoption in the Italian banking sector.

    • Define security, resilience, and compliance requirements for financial institutions using cloud services.

  • Codice in Materia di Protezione dei Dati Personali (Privacy Code)

    • National integration of GDPR, establishing additional data protection rules in Italy.

Germany

  • BaFin Cloud Guidelines

    • Issued by BaFin (Federal Financial Supervisory Authority).

    • Establish cloud security requirements for German financial institutions.

  • BSI-Standard 200-2 (IT-Grundschutz)

    • Developed by BSI (Federal Office for Information Security).

    • Defines IT security standards for financial services, including cloud computing policies.

United Kingdom

  • PRA Cloud Guidance

    • Issued by the Prudential Regulation Authority (PRA).

    • Provides rules and risk assessments for cloud adoption in financial institutions.

  • FCA Cloud Outsourcing Guidelines

    • Released by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA).

    • Regulate cloud outsourcing, requiring continuous monitoring, governance, and security controls.


France

  • Autorité de Contrôle Prudentiel et de Résolution (ACPR) Cloud Guidelines

    • Enforces risk management policies for cloud adoption in French financial institutions.

    • Ensures compliance with European financial regulations.

  • SecNumCloud Certification (ANSSI)

    • A national certification defining security standards for cloud services used by French public and private financial organizations.

Spain

  • Banco de España ICT and Cloud Guidelines

    • Establishes rules for cloud service providers interacting with Spanish financial institutions.

    • Aligns with EBA (European Banking Authority) regulations.

Sweden

  • Finansinspektionen Cloud Regulations

    • Issued by Sweden’s Financial Supervisory Authority.

    • Provides security and compliance requirements for banks adopting cloud services .

China

  • Cybersecurity Law (CSL)

    • Imposes strict data localization requirements, preventing sensitive financial data from being stored outside China.

  • MLPS 2.0 (Multi-Level Protection Scheme)

    • Establishes cybersecurity and cloud security compliance levels for financial services.

Japan

  • FSA Cloud Guidelines (Financial Services Agency)

    • Regulate cloud adoption in the Japanese banking sector.

    • Require disaster recovery, encryption, and risk assessment for financial cloud services.

Singapore

  • Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) Cloud Guidelines

    • Set cloud security and risk management frameworks for financial institutions operating in Singapore.


Canada

  • Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions (OSFI) Cloud Guidelines

    • Establish regulatory requirements for Canadian financial institutions outsourcing to cloud providers.

  • Mandate third-party risk management and security assessments.

These national regulations reflect the growing need for country-specific compliance in cloud-based financial services, ensuring data protection, resilience, and legal alignment across jurisdictions.

Industry-Specific Regulations for the Banking and Financial Sector

These regulations are specifically designed for the banking and financial industry, with a strong emphasis on risk management and cloud security.

  • Basel Accords (Basilea I, II, III & IV)

    • International banking regulations that establish capital requirements, risk management frameworks, and cybersecurity mandates.

    • Their principles influence cloud adoption strategies, particularly for data protection and operational resilience.

  • ISO 27017 (Cloud Security Controls)

    • International security standard providing guidelines for cloud service providers (CSPs) and cloud customers.

    • Ensures best practices for risk management and compliance in cloud-based financial environments.

  • ISO 27018 (Protection of Personal Data in the Cloud)

    • Focuses on the protection of personally identifiable information (PII) in public cloud environments.

    • Helps financial institutions ensure GDPR and data privacy compliance when using cloud services.

  • PCI-DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard)

    • Mandatory security standard for organizations handling credit card transactions.

    • Requires secure data storage, encryption, and network protection in cloud-based payment systems.

  • SWIFT CSP (Customer Security Programme)

    • Security framework for financial institutions using the SWIFT network.

    • Mandates cybersecurity controls and cloud security measures for banks processing international transactions.


Cloud Compliance and Responsibility Matrix

When evaluating a cloud provider, financial institutions must assess compliance with sector-specific regulations that apply to their operations.

Key Considerations for Cloud Compliance

  • For public cloud adoption – The cloud provider must demonstrate compliance with the relevant banking and financial security regulations.

  • For private or community cloud models – The owning organizations must self-declare adherence to industry regulations to protect end users.

A cloud ecosystem consists of thousands of cloud resources, often deployed using hybrid and multi-cloud service models.

Each cloud resource follows a specific service model (IaaS, PaaS, SaaS, etc.), which defines the responsibility split between the cloud provider and the customer.

Understanding the responsibility matrix is crucial in determining the boundary between:

  • The primary cloud provider’s responsibility (security, compliance, infrastructure)

  • The financial institution’s responsibility (data protection, configuration, governance)

Ultimately, financial regulations aim to protect the end-user’s rights and ensure financial data security.

Even in this context, the ISO/OSI stack can serve as a useful reference for understanding and defining the responsibility chain in cloud-based financial services.
